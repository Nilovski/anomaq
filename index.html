Pipeline

Four quantum methods, one consensus

AnomaQ runs tabular, image, and graph data through a suite of quantum algorithms simultaneously. Classical methods provide the baseline; quantum methods catch what they miss.

01
Ingest: CSV, image, or graph
AnomaQ accepts numeric tabular data (CSV), image datasets (encoded as per-pixel feature vectors or color-coded attributes), and graph-structured data (adjacency matrices for social network or connectivity analysis). All inputs are normalized via magnitude-preserving amplitude encoding so relative distances survive the quantum embedding.
02
Quantum kernel embedding
Features are mapped into a high-dimensional Hilbert space using a ZZFeatureMap (or Pauli map). The fidelity Gram matrix Kij = |⟨φ(xi)|φ(xj)⟩|² captures inter-feature correlations that no classical kernel can represent — including entanglement-style dependencies across attributes.
03
Four algorithms, four lenses
Quantum PCA extracts eigenvectors of the data density matrix. Quantum Autoencoder measures compression fidelity — low fidelity signals anomaly. QAOA MaxCut partitions the similarity graph to find minority samples. Quantum Kernel OCSVM draws a decision boundary in quantum feature space. Each method captures a different geometry of anomaly.
04
Consensus ranking + visual explanation
Results from all four quantum methods and three classical baselines are aggregated into a ranked list. Entities flagged by quantum-only methods are visualized with per-feature radar charts — explaining which dimensions drove the detection and why classical methods missed it.

HOW IT WORKS
QAOA MaxCut assigns one qubit per node. The cost Hamiltonian HC = Σ wij/2 (ZiZj − I) is maximized over the graph of pairwise dissimilarities. Nodes landing in the minority partition of the cut are flagged as anomalies — isolating bad actors, bots, or compromised accounts that behave unlike the majority of the network.

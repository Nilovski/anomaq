How it works

Three steps to quantum clarity

No setup. Drop your CSV and AnomaQ runs six classical detectors alongside a full quantum kernel — simultaneously.

01
Drop your CSV
Numeric columns are auto-detected. Text, ID columns and NaN rows are skipped automatically. Works best with 10–500 rows and up to 20 features.
02
Six classical algorithms run in parallel
Isolation Forest, Local Outlier Factor, One-Class SVM — each run twice: on raw features and polynomial interactions. Every outlier is scored per method.
03
Quantum kernel sees what classical can't
The ZZFeatureMap encodes data into quantum state space. The resulting kernel matrix captures correlations that live in exponentially high-dimensional Hilbert space — invisible to every classical detector.

References

  • Lloyd, S., Mohseni, M. & Rebentrost, P. "Quantum principal component analysis." Nature Physics 10, 631–633 (2014). doi:10.1038/nphys3029 — original qPCA algorithm with proven exponential speedup under fault-tolerant quantum hardware + qRAM.
  • Romero, J., Olson, J. P. & Aspuru-Guzik, A. "Quantum autoencoders for efficient compression of quantum data." Quantum Science and Technology 2, 045001 (2017). doi:10.1088/2058-9565/aa8072 — original quantum-autoencoder formulation we adapted for outlier scoring.
  • Corli, S., Moro, L., Dragoni, M., Dispenza, M. & Prati, E. "Quantum machine learning algorithms for anomaly detection: a review." arXiv:2408.11047 (2024). arxiv.org/abs/2408.11047 — recent survey of quantum AD methods, including the two we benchmarked.
  • Han, S., Hu, X., Huang, H., Jiang, M. & Zhao, Y. "ADBench: Anomaly detection benchmark." NeurIPS 2022. github.com/Minqi824/ADBench — the packaged benchmark suite we used for these five datasets.
  • Rayana, S. "ODDS Library." Stony Brook University (2016). odds.cs.stonybrook.edu — original outlier-detection benchmark library upstream of ADBench.
